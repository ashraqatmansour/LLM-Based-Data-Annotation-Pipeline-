

1. Complaints: posts about late grades, decreased university performance, hard exams, exams being lost, 

Examples (what’s in)
* Posts complaining about how late grades are 
* Posts critiquing university’s reputation 
* Posts sharing dissatisfaction and disappointment with exam difficulties 

Edge cases
* Posts mentioning grades without strong negativity -> Grades
* Posts asking neutral questions about policies -> Administration
* Rants about a specific course’s workload without emotional tone -> Courses
* Posts complaining about job/internship market -> Student Life


2. Courses: posts about course recommendations,  unemotional discussion of course difficult 

Examples (what’s in)
* Asking for easy courses 
* Asking for optimal combinations of courses 
* Asking about how a course was with a specific professor 


Edge cases
* Posts focused on exam difficulty -> Exams
* Posts asking whether a course is required for graduation -> Administration
* Asking about when courses are offered -> Administration 
* Posts mainly about grades received in a course -> Grades


3. Student life posts about class group chats, where to meet friends, platforms to use for organization and planning , student events, and student internships 

Examples (what’s in)
* Asking about or recommending tools and apps for student to use to make friends or to organize degree “Building a tool for spontaneous hangouts at Concordia”
* Discussing or asking about clubs and their events on campus 
* Asking for peer’s opinions on student life related matters like clubs, and groups of people 
* Talking about budgeting and accommodation 
* Posts where user is looking for friends, participants or professionals 

Edge cases
* Complaints about social issues (toxicity, exclusion) -> Complaints
* Lost items or logistics-only posts -> Administration

4. Textbooks posts about textbooks needed for courses the post mention title and editions 

Examples (what’s in)
* What textbook is sufficient for a course 
* Posts asking if a textbook edition is valid for a course 
* Users asking for where to find textbooks 


Edge cases
* Posts about course difficulty mentioning textbooks briefly -> Courses


5. Graduate posts about masters programs, phd programs, acceptance rate, perquisites  

Examples (what’s in)
* Posts about specific graduate programs 
* Users seeking advice directly related to graduate level information 
* Posts about courses and grades grades required for grad school 

Edge cases
* Posts about reference letters logistics -> Administration
* Posts asking for career advice without grad school focus -> Advice

6. Administration posts about logistical matters like applying, graduation requirements , lost items

Examples (what’s in)
* How to apply to undergraduate programs and deadlines 
* Graduation requirements and procedure 
* Student fees and payment deadlines 
* Opening hours for buildings and school start/finish date 
* Posts about waitlists for specific courses
* How to switch programs 
* Posts about what a passing grade is 

Edge cases
* Complaints about admin delays -> Complaints


7. Advice posts about changing majors, what to do in specific situations 

Examples (what’s in)
* Posts seeking advice without many details about specifics of the situation 
* Asking what to do if a certain grade was achieved in a class 

Edge cases
* Advice strictly about course difficulty -> Courses
* Advice about grad school decisions -> Graduate


8. Grades posts about when grades come out, what grade is a pass, what gives grade give credits 

Examples (what’s in)
* Announcing that grades are posted 
* Sharing what grade was achieved in a class 
* Asking about when grades will be received 

Edge cases
* Emotional reactions to grades -> Complaints
* Grade requirements for program transfer → Administration
